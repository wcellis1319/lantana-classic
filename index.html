Who We Are

Standards-based health IT, built for the federal government.

Lantana Consulting Group is a 130-person, 100% employee-owned health IT firm that has spent two decades building the federal government's healthcare data infrastructure. We co-authored the HL7 and FHIR standards that every major hospital, insurer, and federal agency now relies on.

We are one of only two small businesses awarded a prime contract on CMS's $800M MIDS IDIQ. Our open-source tools are actively used by CDC, NCI, and 1,100+ health IT professionals worldwide.

As a Certified B Corp and Vermont Benefit Corporation, we are legally and structurally committed to mission over profit — a combination of technical depth, federal trust, and institutional accountability that is essentially unique in the health IT contracting market.

Employee Ownership

100% employee-owned. Every eligible staff member has a stake.

Lantana became a 100% Employee Stock Ownership Plan (ESOP) company in January 2023. Every eligible employee holds an ownership stake — not as a perk, but as a structural commitment to the people who do the work.

For federal contract evaluators, ESOP means something concrete: our people stay. Staff retention, institutional continuity, and mission alignment are built into our ownership structure — not managed through HR programs.

ESOP companies report 92% higher median household net wealth, 33% higher median income from wages, and 53% longer median job tenure than non-ESOP peers (NCEO, 2017).

Nursing & Long-Term Care

The firm that built NHSN's reporting infrastructure.

Lantana built the first CDA application for CDC/NHSN and has held continuous NHSN contracts since 2007 — spanning HAI surveillance, antimicrobial use and resistance reporting, nursing home COVID-19 vaccine tracking, and FHIR-based automated reporting.

Our nursing staff, epidemiologists, and public health analysts don't translate clinical requirements — they write them. Published in JAMIA and co-authors of CDC MMWR studies, Lantana's NHSN domain knowledge is peer-reviewed and federally validated.

HAI Surveillance Infrastructure

Built and maintained the electronic reporting infrastructure for hospital-acquired infection surveillance across hospitals, dialysis centers, and long-term care facilities.

NHSNLink — Automated Reporting

CDC MMWR Co-Authorship

Standards Authority

Supporting ONC's USCDI standard development and implementation. Ensuring federal health data requirements translate into working systems.

QRDA
